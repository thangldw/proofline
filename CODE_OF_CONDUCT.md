# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Proofline contributors, maintainers, and community participants are expected to
help create a respectful, harassment-free environment regardless of age, body
size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression,
experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a healthy community include:

- showing empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experience levels;
- giving specific, actionable feedback about work rather than people;
- accepting responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes;
- protecting private source data, credentials, and vulnerability details; and
- focusing discussion on the project's evidence-first scope and community needs.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, insults, sexualized language
or attention, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, publishing private information,
and sustained disruption of project spaces.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
project events, and private communications made in an official project role.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns through the maintainer's private profile-contact path
described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), clearly identifying the message as a
Code of Conduct report rather than a vulnerability. Do not use a vulnerability
form for conduct reports and do not post sensitive evidence publicly.

Maintainers must handle reports confidentially, disclose conflicts of interest,
and recuse themselves when appropriate. Proportionate responses may include a
private clarification, warning, temporary participation restriction, removal of
content, or a permanent ban. Retaliation against a good-faith reporter is not
permitted. During pre-alpha there is no guaranteed response-time SLA.
